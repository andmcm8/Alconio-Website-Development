Understanding Metrics

Metrics are the lifeblood of Alconio. They tell the story of how your users interact with your application and how your infrastructure responds. This guide defines common terms you'll encounter throughout the dashboard.

Core Metrics

  • Total Sessions: The total number of unique visits to your application within the selected timeframe.
  • New vs. Returning: A breakdown of users visiting for the first time versus those who have visited before.
  • Bounce Rate: The percentage of sessions that result in only one page view, indicating a lack of engagement.

Engagement Signals

How do you know if your users are truly engaged? We use several indicators:

  • Avg. Session Duration: The mean time a user spends on your site per session.
  • Pages per Session: The average number of distinct pages viewed by a user.
  • Stickiness: A ratio calculated by dividing Daily Active Users (DAU) by Monthly Active Users (MAU), showing how often users return.

Performance Metrics (Core Web Vitals)

Alconio tracks your site's health through Google's Core Web Vitals:

  • LCP (Largest Contentful Paint): Measures loading performance. Aim for 2.5 seconds or less.
  • FID (First Input Delay): Measures interactivity. Aim for 100 milliseconds or less.
  • CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift): Measures visual stability. Aim for 0.1 or less.

Custom Events & Conversions

Beyond standard tracking, you can define what "Success" means for your app. Whether it's a signup, a purchase, or a video play, Alconio lets you track these as Custom Events. When an event represents a business goal, you can mark it as a Conversion to track conversion rates over time.
